Monsters

As the name of the series implies, Monster Hunter is a series where the player is tasked with hunting various monsters. There are over 200 monsters including subspecies, variants, and deviants. Each monster has a distinctive feature that allows it to stand out from others. Some of these monsters can range from a large bear, Arzuros, to a legendary black dragon, Fatalis, that was said to have wiped out an entire county in one night. Monsters tend to be territorial, and recent games allow them to shake things up by duking it out with another monster during a hunter's quest. Every monster has a weakness to different elements, and certain weapons work better on different parts of the monsters. Researching these creatures and finding out what monster is weakest to what attack is key to completing your quest.

Locales

Knowing the location of your hunt can greatly increase the chances of success. Each location has unique features such as active volcanos, scorching deserts, humid swamps, freezing tundras, and lush forests. The monsters will use the enviorment to their advantage. This can from springing off of walls to crush the hunter, to pulling ancient weapons out of the ground to pierce their foes. In recent games, hunters have been able to use the enviorment aginst the monsters. Using natural traps like vines, or falling rocks, hunters can open up opportunities to unleash an all-out attack on the monster. Memorizing the world around you can make the difference between life and death.
